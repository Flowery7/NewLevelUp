Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to the most common questions about scaffolding services in Surrey

The duration depends on your project type. Most residential projects require scaffolding for 2-6 weeks. Roof repairs typically take 1-3 weeks, while larger projects like extensions or rendering can take 4-8 weeks. We provide a detailed timeline during our initial consultation and can adjust as needed.

Generally, no planning permission is required for temporary scaffolding. However, if the scaffolding will be on public property like pavements or roads, you'll need a license from your local council. We handle all necessary permits and licenses as part of our service, ensuring full compliance with local regulations.

Safety is our top priority. All our scaffolding complies with UK health and safety regulations (BS EN 12811). We conduct daily inspections, provide safety certificates, use edge protection systems, install warning signs, and ensure all our team members are CISRS certified. We also carry £10 million public liability insurance for your peace of mind.

When installed by professionals like Level Up Scaffolding, the risk of damage is minimal. We use protective measures including base plates, rubber feet, and wall pads to prevent damage to surfaces. Our experienced team takes great care during installation and dismantling. Any rare instances of damage are covered by our comprehensive insurance.

We cover all of Surrey and surrounding areas including Guildford, Woking, Farnham, Godalming, Haslemere, Dorking, Reigate, Redhill, and beyond. Whether you're in urban Guildford or rural Surrey villages, our teams can reach you. Contact us to confirm coverage for your specific location.

Scaffolding costs vary based on project size, duration, and complexity. A standard semi-detached house might cost £800-£1,200 for 2-3 weeks, while larger properties or complex installations can range from £1,500-£3,000+. We provide transparent, fixed-price quotes with no hidden costs after a free site survey.
